Missionary Work

Mother Teresa dedicated her life to helping the poor and the sick. She founded the Missionaries of Charity in Calcutta, India, in 1950. Her organization provided care and support to those in need, including the homeless, the sick, and the dying.

Nobel Peace Prize

In 1979, Mother Teresa was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for her selfless dedication to humanitarian work. She continued her mission of love and care until her passing in 1997.

Legacy

Mother Teresa's legacy continues to inspire people around the world to serve the less fortunate. She was canonized as a saint by the Catholic Church in 2016.
